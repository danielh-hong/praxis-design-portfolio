About me

Hey! My name is Daniel and I'm an Engineering Science Student at the University of Toronto. Life to me is all about pushing my limits and becoming a better version of myself. What I love about engineering is learning new concepts and applying what I learn to create real, tangible items.



Currently, I have strong interests in aerospace engineering, robotics, machine learning, and software development. I am hoping to specialize in one of these fields in my third year.

As of now, I am an active member of UofT's Aerospace Design Team (UTAT) and Wind Design Team (UWind) where I am developing my hands-on skills. In UTAT, I am currently working on the design of a quadcopter and have so far designed the stringers and bulkheads of the fuselage using Solidworks. In UTWind, I have designed the nacelle of the wind turbine, which will house the generator and gearbox.

Outside of engineering, I am a musician, athlete, and hobbyiest photographer. I have over 10 years of experience playing the piano and violin, and was a member of UofT's Varsity Track and Field Team. At my best, I cleared 190 cm in high jump and achieved top 30 national rankings. Unfortunately, I am currently recovering from a hamstring tear.

I truly believe that life is all about creating the best version of myself, and this core extends to all facets of my life. I'm always trying to improve my health, my mind, and my abilities, and will continue to do so for the rest of my life.
